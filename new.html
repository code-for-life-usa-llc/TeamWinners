Why Games are good for you

For years, video games have often been criticized for being a waste of time. But recent studies show that many games can actually be good for your brain—enhancing memory, attention, creativity, and even emotional well-being. While moderation is still key, here are some types of video games that can give your brain a real workout.

Games that involve solving puzzles or planning strategies are excellent for strengthening your cognitive skills. Classics like Tetris have been shown to improve spatial reasoning and even reduce unwanted flashbacks and cravings. Meanwhile, titles like Portal and Portal 2 require players to use creative thinking and logic to solve complex challenges in a 3D space.

For more targeted mental training, apps like Lumosity, Peak, and Elevate offer short, scientifically designed exercises that help improve memory, attention, and problem-solving skills. Nintendo’s Brain Age series and mobile versions of Sudoku offer similar benefits in a gamified format.

While they often get a bad rap, fast-paced action games can actually sharpen the brain. Research shows that games like Call of Duty or Battlefield can improve visual attention, decision-making speed, and multitasking. These games require players to monitor multiple elements on-screen, react quickly, and make split-second decisions.
